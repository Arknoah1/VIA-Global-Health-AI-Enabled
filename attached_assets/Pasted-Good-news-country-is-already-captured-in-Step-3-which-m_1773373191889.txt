## What This Changes

The 4-step flow now collects: **Product → Quantity → Name + Email + Country → (Step 4, unseen)**. That's a solid foundation. The one glaring gap remains **org type**, and this screen actually reveals *where* to fix it most elegantly.

## Revised Recommendations

### 1. Add Org Type to Step 3 — It Belongs Right Here ✅ Easy Win

Step 3 already has the right framing (*"so I can calculate shipping and finalise your quote"*). Org type directly affects the final price, so it belongs on the same screen. Add a simple segmented selector or dropdown between Email and Shipping Country:

> **Organisation Type**
> `[ NGO / Charity ] [ Government / Public Hospital ] [ Healthcare Provider ] [ Distributor ] [ Private Clinic ]`

This single addition eliminates the most common conversation-killing loop in the dataset — and it fits naturally without making the form feel longer.

---

### 2. The Email Placeholder is Doing Conversion Work for You — Lean Into It

The placeholder `jane@clinic.com` is a subtle but smart signal that this is a professional/institutional context. Consider extending that logic to the **org type field** with a placeholder like *"e.g. NGO, Government Hospital, Distributor"* — it pre-qualifies visitors and helps borderline cases self-select correctly before hitting Amara.

---

### 3. Step 2 Still Needs a Volume Nudge

The bare quantity input remains unchanged — and for high-unit-cost products where freight can exceed product cost at low quantities, this is still a missed opportunity. A single line below the input field:

> *"Most organisations order 5–10 units. Volume discounts applied automatically."*

This sets a social norm anchor and nudges toward order sizes where the economics make sense.

---

### 4. Step 1 Pricing Framing Remains the Priority

Leading with `$2,100.00 per unit` as a hard number still risks filtering out NGO and government buyers — your most eligible segment — before they even enter the funnel. This is now the **highest-impact change that hasn't been addressed** in the UI. Softening it to a range with a subsidy signal costs nothing and could meaningfully improve Step 1 → Step 2 dropoff.

---

### 5. Step 4 is the Unknown Variable

We haven't seen Step 4 yet — but given the flow, it's likely either a confirmation screen or the chat opening. If it's a confirmation screen, that's where a **"your subsidised rate will be applied"** message could reinforce the value of completing the form. If the chat opens directly, Amara should greet them with a near-complete quote in turn one, not a question.
